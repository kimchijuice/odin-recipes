Nilagang Baka

Description

Nilagang baka is a Filipino beef stew that's known for its hearty and comforting flavor. It's typically made with a beef shank or brisket, which is slowly simmered in a broth that's often flavored with vegetables like potatoes, carrots, and corn. The broth is usually seasoned with salt, pepper, and sometimes a combination of fish sauce and soy sauce. Other ingredients that can be added to the stew include onions, garlic, ginger, and bay leaves. The stew is often served with rice or noodles. Nilagang baka is a popular dish in the Philippines, especially during cooler weather. It's a classic example of Filipino cuisine, combining influences from Spanish and Chinese cooking.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 kg beef laman
  • 1 big onion - quartered
  • 8 cups of water
  • 10 pieces peppercorn
  • 4 bay leaf
  • 1 star anise
  • 1 big potato
  • 9 saba
  • 15 pcs green beans
  • 1 small cabbage

Steps

  1. Sear beef until brown. Remove. Then, saute onions.
  2. Add the beef back. Pour the water and add star anise, bay leaf, and perppercorns.
  3. Pressure cook the meat for 30 mins.
  4. Add the potatoes, beans, and saba. Season with salt and fish sauce. Cook covered for another 10 minutes or until the potatoes are cooked.
  5. Add the cabbage and then turn off the heat. Cover with lid and let the vegetables cook for 2-3 minutes.
